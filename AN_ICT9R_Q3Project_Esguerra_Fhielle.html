5 ways to avoid fire accidents


Don't leave the kitchen when you're cooking.

Always keep an eye on food while it's cooking by following these rules: Stay in the kitchen while the food is cooking.Avoid putting things that easily catch fire or burn close to your stove, such as towels and paper.

Keep your wires in order

Learn to see the red flags that indicate to a potential wiring or electrical issue to avoid an electrical fire. If you experience any of the difficulties in your house, get in touch with a qualified electrician right away to inspect and fix electrical issues.

Keep Flammable items in the proper area.

Make sure that every container being stored is intact, closed, and labeled correctly. To avoid the formation of combustible vapor/air combinations or the inhalation of harmful vapors or gases, use flammable and combustible liquids in a fume hood. Avoid placing anything that can ignite, such as hot objects, fires, or sparking machinery, near these liquids. If at all possible, utilize electrical heating in place of open fires.


Check the Heating devices

. The majority of labs employ at least one form of heating apparatus, including microwave ovens, hot plates, heating mantles and tapes, oil baths, salt baths, sand baths, and air baths.

Have a fire extinguisher at all times

The fire blanket should be briefly compressed down all around to seal off the fire source. Keep the extinguishing blanket on the fire's source to prevent re-ignition.
